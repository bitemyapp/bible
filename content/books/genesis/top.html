Genesis In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth. In the beginning God created heaven, and earth. "heaven" here is samayim, 9028/8026 in Strong's Hebrew concordance. Morphologically it's plural so "the heavens" seemed more literal here although "heaven and earth" has nicer prosody. No comma because I have no idea what that is accomplishing. "earth" is singular absolute. And the earth was formless and void, and darkness was upon the surface of the deep; and the spirit of God moved over the waters. And the earth was formless and void, and darkness was upon the face of the abyss; and the spirit of God moved over the waters. And the earth was void and empty, and darkness was upon the face of the deep; and the spirit of God moved over the waters. The earth was without form and void, and darkness was upon the face of the deep; and the Spirit of God was moving over the face of the waters. But the earth was unseen and unprepared, and darkness was upon the deep. And the spirit of God rushed upon the water. The Septuagint's Greek here seems to be something like "invisible" and "un-formed". "Deep" in the Greek is abyss. I was tempted to go with "empty" instead of void here but empty implies a "container" for the emptiness whereas void conveys uncreatedness/un-formedness better in English. I believe abyss is clearer to modern English speakers here than an antiquated use of "deep" that is evocative of the ocean deep. This could be clarified by using surface rather than face in combination with "deep" which is true to the Hebrew.
